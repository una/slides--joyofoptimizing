Lossless v. Lossy

- PNG is an example of lossless compression, it doesn't lose quality. - PNG uses delta compression - difference of one pixel to another (bc most images have large areas of similar color) - chooses the pixel closest in value (hoping to be 0) which decreases overall file size - jpg also looks at difference of one pixel to another and discards the excess (it rounds where it can) - everytime you resave a jpg, even at 100% quality, it will lose detail

Tests on photographic images in July 2014 found that BPG produced smaller files for a given quality than JPEG, JPEG XR and WebP
